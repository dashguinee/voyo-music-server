Light Bloom (Physical Glow Spread)

Real neon creates three layers of glow: sharp core, medium bloom, and soft diffuse halo. Power level affects bloom radius—higher power = larger glow spread. This mimics light scattering through air and lens aberration.

Weather Effects

Moisture and rain cause significant optical changes. Water droplets scatter light unpredictably, creating larger halos and reducing color saturation. Fog diffuses the glow so much the sign almost disappears. Chromatic aberration (color fringing) occurs from light refraction through water.

Technical Reference & Physics

Color Physics

Gas Wavelengths (Physics)
Neon (Pure): 600-650nm (Red-Orange) → Warm, nostalgic Argon (Pure): 420nm (Pale Blue) → Cold, requires phosphor Helium: 587nm (Yellow) → Warm, rare Xenon: 175nm (Deep Lavender) → Very cold, rare Phosphor-Coated: 400-700nm (Any color) → Depends on coating Key: Shorter wavelength = cooler feel, longer = warmer feel

Text-Shadow Layers

Three-Layer Bloom Structure
Layer 1 - Core Glow: 0-10px → Sharp, intense, full saturation Layer 2 - Mid Bloom: 15-30px → Medium blur, 0.5-0.8 opacity Layer 3 - Outer Halo: 40-80px+ → Very soft, 0.1-0.4 opacity Real Neon Parameters: • Temperature Shift: Warm = longer shadows, Cold = sharper shadows • Power Level: 2-3x bloom radius increase with higher voltage • Degradation: Each failure mode reduces bloom symmetry

Animation Timing

Real-World Durations
